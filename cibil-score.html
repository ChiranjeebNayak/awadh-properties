What is a CIBIL score?

To put it simply, a CIBIL score is a score for a consumer’s credits. A CIBIL score is a blueprint of a consumer’s credit records and overall credit profile. This is inclusive of the repayment and borrowing activities of a consumer given to CIBIL by the credit lenders and banks. Now, you would be wondering what is CIBIL? So, here it is. CIBIL is a company for credit information, established in 2000 that holds the largest data of consumer information.

Because a CIBIL score is something that cannot be modified, changed, or altered otherwise, a consistent CIBIL score check is advised. The ‘inquiries’ and ‘accounts’ sections in a CIBIL report have information that forms a CIBIL score. Your payment statuses, outstanding bills, loan accounts, and credit card information is all present in a CIBIL report. CIBIL score is a three-digit number ranging from 300-900. The closer you are to 900, the better are your chances of credit approvals and vice versa. Since, your past financial habits indicate your prospects, maintaining a good CIBIL score is always beneficial. Also, you can have a CIBIL score check free on various online platforms.

What is a CIBIL report?

To define a CIBIL report, it is a consolidation of a consumer’s credit history, loan accounts, employment information, personal information, and contact details. You need to know that the CIBIL report is what forms your CIBIL score. Hence, a clean CIBIL report and a good CIBIL score will take you through a credit or loan approval.

Its Importance:

  • All the lending institutions can get to know about your creditworthiness.
  • A CIBIL report helps in getting easy access to the loans given by banks.
  • You inculcate financial discipline by keeping a consistent CIBIL score report.
  • You will be saved from falling into a debt trap
  • You become eligible to do interest rate negotiations after reaching a 750 CIBIL score.

Components of a CIBIL report

A CIBIL report contains sections with detailed information on a consumer’s financial history. Have a look at which sections are included:

CIBIL Score: Your CIBIL report is incomplete without a CIBIL score which is determined by the data present in the accounts and inquiries section.

Personal Information: Here all your identification numbers like PAN, voters ID, and passport number will be mentioned along with your DOB, name, and gender.

Contact Details: Your phone number and addresses will be listed in this section. Up to 4 addresses can be added.

Employment Information: This section will have the details of your monthly and annual income as given out by the banks and financial institutions.

Account Information: It has multiple things covered. Name of your lending institutions, types of credit services taken, date of account opening, last payment date, loan amount information, balance, monthly records of your transactions, and ownership details.

Inquiry Information: When you apply for credits in a financial institution, it will scan your CIBIL report. All your applications and inquiries about loans will be listed along with the name of the vendor, amount, date, and type of credit requested.

How is CIBIL score calculated?

Your creditworthiness or CIBIL score is calculated from a range of 300-900 by the TransUnion CIBIL bureau. Here are the factors which make up your overall CIBIL score:

Your payment history

One of the biggest factors that can positively or negatively affect your CIBIL score is your payment history. If you are regular with paying your outstanding balances of the credit cards or loan EMIs, it gives a boost to your CIBIL score. The vice-versa is also true. If you neglect, miss, or delay your payments, it can bring down your CIBIL score.

Credit Utilization

Subsequently, your credit exposure or credit utilization ratio is what affects your CIBIL score calculation. It is a ratio of the credit used proportioning your available credit limit. According to financial experts, it is advised to use up to 30% of your total credit card limit. If you have high credit exposure, it shows your credit hunger and struggles with credits. With more usage of credit, it gives an impression to the lenders that you can turn into a defaulter which leads to rejection. So, no chances of an instant loan without CIBIL.

Type of credit and its duration

When you have a long history of credit, it shows that you are good at handling credits. The number of years that have been passed after the opening of your first credit account is referred to as credit history. Experts say that it is good to maintain a credit history and start using credits at a young age so that in the future it is easy to build up a CIBIL score when you want to purchase a home or car. The type of credits that you take also plays a part in CIBIL score calculation. One should try to keep a balance of unsecured and secured loans. Having both secured loans like home or auto loans and unsecured loans like a credit card shows that you have experience in handling both.

        What are CIBIL bureaus?

        To put it simply,CIBIL bureaus are the agencies collecting the data about a consumer’s credits, outstanding payments, billing transactions, borrowing, and lending. All of this information is then passed onto the credit information companies that assess an individual’s CIBIL report and credit statement for the approval of loans and credits.

        The CIBIL bureaus collect this data from various public record offices, lending institutions, creditors, or some debt collection organizations. These reports may go comprehensive and include your phone bills and utility bills. This information is further compiled into a CIBIL report. Both the individuals and the creditors can have access to the CIBIL report in exchange for a small fee. However, any CIBIL bureau cannot influence the approval or rejection of credit or loan to an individual.

CIBIL score ranges from 300-900 and any score above 850 is regarded to be exceptional and a reflection of disciplined financial behavior.

        CIBIL rank

        It is a historical record of all the loans taken by a company along with their repayment schedules. A CIBIL rank is given in between 1 to 10. The businesses with a CIBIL rank of 1 are the most preferred borrowers and businesses with a CIBIL rank of 10 are not the ones accepted by lenders. Thus, the businesses having a CIBIL rank closer to 1 have better chances of getting approved for fresh credits in comparison to the ones nearing a CIBIL rank of 10.

        Cibil Score Range And What It Means

        Let’s delve deep into the excellent, average, and low CIBIL scores and how they can impact an individual.

        A range of 750-900

        A CIBIL score under this bracket is known to be excellent and the chances of getting a loan are the highest. Adding more to it, the process of loan approval is facilitated and works without any hassle. A CIBIL score between 750-900 showcases the creditworthiness of the consumer which casts an impression of a good quality borrower in front of the lenders. It also reassures the lender that the retrieval of the loan will be on time. A score like this presents the borrower with the room to negotiate the amount of loan and the rate of interest to choose the prospective one.

        A range of 700-749

        Usually, most individuals have a CIBIL score between the 700-749 range which is regarded as a good CIBIL score. Consumers have a fairly high chance of getting their loan applications approved. Though the score in this range is dependable, it is not as risk-free as a score lying between 750-900. Thus, the chances of loan approval are less in comparison to a 750 and above CIBIL score. Consumers with this CIBIL score range should work on improving their score.

        A range of 650-699

        A CIBIL score in the range of 650-699 is considered to be average and shows that an individual is neither on time nor too delayed for debt payments. However, there is room for improving the CIBIL score by maintaining good credit behavior to avoid any loan rejections. When an individual has a middling score between 650-699, it is difficult for them to have their loans approved with the required terms and conditions. The consumer will have to face a high rate of interest, high premiums, and no additional benefits. Be strict with your credit card payments and loan installments to not decrease your CIBIL score any further.

        A range of 550-649

        If you are thinking of getting your loan approved with a CIBIL score between 550-649, then be ready to face some rejections. Approving the loans for this CIBIL score range can put the lenders at great risk of losing their money. This range of CIBIL score represents that the borrower has been consistent with defaulting repayments of loans and credit cards and showcases an unstable financial behavior. It also minimizes the trust of the lender regarding the repayment of the amount on the due date. At times, the lenders agree to sanction the loan but with a guarantor so that in case the borrower defaults on the payment, it can be received from the guarantor. To improve this CIBIL score, the borrowers need to stop applying for loans or using the credit card till the complete outstanding amount is paid.

        A range of 550 or below

        A CIBL score below 550 makes the chances of getting a loan approval nil as it is a reflection of poor credit history. Apart from loan rejections, the borrower will be offered a small amount of loan at a very high rate of interest. The borrowers can also be asked to take their loans against shares, assets, FDs, or gold. Keep yourself prepared that getting a loan with a CIBIL score falling under 550 is next to impossible and the score can be improved only with a strict and consistent financial discipline.

        How the CIBIL score impacts loan eligibility!

        Impact on Home Loan

        A good CIBIL score is highly crucial to get your home loan approved. If your CIBIL score is not up to the mark, you can end up paying high rates of interest or even suffer loan rejections. Your CIBIL score is generated through the evaluation of your financial history which is compiled in the CIBIL report. This has to be good because it influences the approval of loans and an overall evaluation of the loan applications. Any CIBIL score above 750 can let you sail through the sea  of home loan approval. Because the CIBIL score and CIBIL score report are the first few things checked by the lenders, they have to be fair, or else the application can get rejected.

        Impact on Auto Loan

        Buying a car is expensive for a majority of people, be it a new one or a second-hand car. Hence, individuals prefer taking a car loan. However,  some lenders offer the loan for the entire value of the car while some offer only for 80% of the car’s value. To get an easier and faster car loan approval, your CIBIL score should be above 750. A good CIBIL score showcases that you have a good financial history which makes the lenders trust you. So, do not forget to do a CIBIL score check before buying a car. If your CIBIL is not up to the mark, work on it with strict financial discipline and you will reach a 750 and above score easily.

        Impact on Personal Loan

        At times in life, you need some extra finance to achieve your long-term and short-term goals. A personal loan is the ideal kind of funding that you need but financial institutions or lenders watch out for certain parameters before granting one. One of the most important factors that they consider is your CIBIL score. Generally, a CIBIL score of 750 or more is considered to be good for getting your application for a personal loan approved. Remember that a good CIBIL score raises a green flag in front of the lenders and a low CIBIL score raises a red flag of poor creditworthiness. 750 and above score can get you a quicker disbursal of funds and low rates of interest. In addition, you can negotiate for more amounts to be sanctioned for a personal loan.

        Types of credit that impact your CIBIL score

        Usually, the type of credit which impacts the CIBIL score is a loan that is of two types- unsecured loans and secured loans. Loans like auto loans or home loans are known to be the secured loans because they are secured with collateral.  Loans like personal loans and a credit card are the ones that can be availed without any collateral. If your credit history has a mix of both secured and unsecured loans, it affects your CIBIL score positively and increases the chances of getting fresh credit. On the other hand, having many unsecured loans or credit cards can affect your CIBIL score negatively. Lenders think of this as a risk of the borrower turning into a defaulter.

        What is the highest CIBIL score one has got to date?

        In India, as per the guidelines of the Reserve Bank of India, 900 is the highest CIBIL score attainable by an individual. Though any score above 800 is considered to be premium, 900 is the epitome of an excellent CIBIL score.

        A CIBIL score of 849 - Is it a good score or a bad score?

        As per the financial experts, a score ranging from 800-900 is considered to be exceptional. Having an 849 CIBIL score is quite above the average credit score of many individuals and you will hardly face any rejections for your credit applications. But, if you still want to improve your CIBIL score from 849, you need to evaluate the components present in your CIBIL score report and see what practices can eliminate them from your report. For your information, with a CIBIL score of 849, the lender will offer you the best deals of the rate of interest as you are least likely to default your repayments. Every credit card issuer will also offer you their best loyalty programs and reward cards.
