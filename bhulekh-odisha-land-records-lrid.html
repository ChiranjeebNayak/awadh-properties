Odisha, a state on India's eastern coast, is divided into 30 districts (Tehsils), or administrative geographical units. To simplify their administration, these 30 districts have been divided into three revenue divisions. Angul is a district in Odisha that is located in the heart of the state. According to the 2011 census, Angul District is densely populated. There are 1930 villages in the district, with a total population of 1273821 people. Angul city holds the administrative headquarters of the Angul District. In the current administrative setup, the District has four major divisions, eight tahsils, and eight squares. The industrial situation in Angul District has improved significantly over the last decade. National Aluminium Company Limited (NALCO), Mahanadi Coal Fields Limited (MCL), National Thermal Power Corporation (NTPC), and Talcher Thermal Power Station(TTPS) are just a few of the public sector companies that have set up shop here. Dhokra casting works, Terracotta works, Wood carvings, Art textiles, and Soft toys, to name a few, are some of the crafts that have brought in money for this district. In terms of educational institutes, Angul District has a significant number of both government and private institutions. The District's significant educational institutions include Government College (Angul), Angul Women's College (Angul), and Talcher College (Talcher).

Land Records Web Portal of Odisha - Odisha Bhulekh

Bhulekh Odisha is an online portal created by the Government of Odisha that allows people of the state (Odisha) to access their land record information. The website set up by and handled by the Department of Revenue, Odisha allows them to get their land record information much easier. The online platform can be used to collect information about the land, the property owner and the property's value.

The procedure to obtain your land record information is easy to follow and has a fast turnaround time.

What are the ROR Documents required?

To apply for an Bhulekh Odisha ROR (Records of Rights) , you must include the documents mentioned below:

  1. Ration Card.
  2. Proof of Identify – Voter ID.
  3. Proof of Address – Aadhaar Card.
  4. Proof of ownership of property.
  5. Land Tax.
  6. Encumbrance certificate.
  7. Evidence for Income Sources – Income certificate.

The Record of Rights (ROR) is a critical document that includes information about the property's true owner. If an Odisha resident wishes to sell or purchase property, he must first obtain a Record of Rights (ROR) paper. The Revenue and Disaster Management Department of the Government of Odisha is in charge of issuing the ROR paper. Names of landowners, history of property, cost of land, and other details are available in the ROR text, as well as types of land, agricultural aspects, and other information.

What is the purpose of Bhulekh Odisha?

Every detail and knowledge related to land is referred to as Bhulekh. Therefore, Bhulekh in Odisha applies to all land-related data in the state of Odisha. All land records in Odisha were maintained manually until 2008. Only the concerned agencies had access to land records. Odisha's government began digitising its land records as part of the National Land Records Modernization Program. The state now maintains all of its land records through the Bhulekh Orissa online portal. Accessing information about lands located in Odisha has become a hassle-free activity for everyone thanks to the digitization of land records.

The wide-ranged services it provides are:

  • Access to Record of Rights
  • Access to maps
  • Displays State Statistics
  • Provides Tehsil information.

With the launch of the Bhulekh Odisha came the following benefits.

  • Transparency in the land record management system of Orissa.
  • Transparency in Bhulekh Orissa Map
  • Conversion from manual to digital operation saves time and reduces the chance of mistakes.
  • Round-the-clock access to land records.
