Total Projects: 921

Are you considered buying property in a city, which is quaint and modern? If yes, there is good news! Why not consider investing in the capital city of Uttar Pradesh – Lucknow also referred as the city of Nawabs? With a rich tradition for legacy, the city is opened to explore the future by adapting to it. Though the real estate boom arrives just now, the property rates in Lucknow have shown an exponential increase in the long term. Lucknow is simply a city, which poised to carve a position for itself in the real estate landscape of the country. Quality educational institutions, good infrastructure, state-of-the-art shopping malls, and healthcare facilities are some features, which have prompted prominent developers to launch mega residential projects in Lucknow including Balaji The Landmark, Paarth Humming State, Urban Axis Urban Woods, Paarth Arka, Excella Kutumb, Viraj Lotus Court, Viraj Lotus Enclave, and Viraj Sun Breeze 2.

The trans-GoMati region is dotted with several locations that are considered to be the realty hotspots. Gomati Nagar, Indira Nagar, and Jankipuram are fastest growing real estate markets found in Lucknow. Because of the radial expansion of the city, the highways connecting Lucknow together with surrounding cities like Hardoi, Sultanpur, Kanpur, Sitapur, Faizabad, and Rai Bareli have been spotting a rapid paced real estate activity. On seeing the growth potential of these regions, private realty investors are now flocking towards these regions in order to get the first mover advantage.

Lucknow properties are characterized by good infrastructural facilities like meticulously planned road networks, better sanitation and water facilities in addition to well-equipped hospitals, organized retails and educational centers. Needless to say, the city is well-connected through different state and national highways in order to give improved connectivity. The Amar Shaheed Path, a 23 km long elevated road, connects Faizabad Road and Kanpur Road by passing through Rai Bareli Road and Sultanpur Road. The Charbagh Junction is considered to be the city’s main railway station. Apart from this, there are thirteen other railway stations found within the city. Chaudhary Charan Singh International airport, located at Amausi, is just 20 km from the Central Business District that connects Lucknow with other metros including Patna, Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai, and Hyderabad.

The Lucknow metro project is expected to be launched very soon by the Lucknow Metro Rail Corporation. According to the plan, the initial phase of the project consists of three corridors namely the North-South Corridor, East-west Corridor, and the Gomtinagar link. Moreover, an IT park has been planned under the aegis of LIDA at the Amausi Industrial area. To foster walk-to-work concept, the IT Park includes service apartments along with office spaces by incorporating almost all the public amenities into properties in Lucknow for sale. This will attract a number of IT multinationals to set up their businesses in the IT Park. Without any doubt, Lucknow is slated as the topmost city in India with enormous real estate potential. These radial expansions have untangled new pockets for both investors and end users to have realty property in Lucknow.
