How to apply for online mutation via Banglarbhumi?

The Banglarbhumi official website has access to automatic mutation. Mutation is the process which allows the transfer of property from one person to another. This is a legal process. However, we can avail this online service only if the property is registered. It can be transferable only if its final publication is complete and the transfer of land is not done on the basis of the holding number. After mutation, the new owner must then start the payment of property tax. For automatic property mutations through Banglarbhumi, there is no need to file an application. (NOTE: All applications are mandatory, and if there is any need to file for corrections in the papers or if there is any objection within 30 days of sale of the land, then one must approach the Block Land and Land Reforms Office (BL and LRO) to file an application.

Is the mutation form available online?

Yes, the mutation form is available online on the official website. One will have to enter the details of the applicant, particulars of the transfer and the scanned copies of the land and tax bills. The application number will be generated, which can be used to check the status of your application. You will have to complete all the procedures and then by paying the stipulated fee for it. You can choose to pay either by debit card or through net banking. Offline fee payment is also allowed. You can check the status of such payment through ‘Application- GRN Search’ from the ‘Online Application’ option. 

Will the payment of the mutation fee vary?

Yes, the fee for mutation is different in different categories. The mutation fee is different for agricultural, non-agricultural, non-commercial land or commercial and industrial land. It also varies, depending upon the location, rural area, municipal area other than the Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority (KMDA) or municipal areas that are within the jurisdiction of the KMDA.

How to apply for land conversion through Banglarbhumi?

Every land that is meant for a particular purpose can be converted to a different purpose after obtaining proper permission through proper procedures. Agricultural, commercial or industrial land can be converted and can be put to any other use. The first step to do so is by submitting an application to the Land and Land Reforms and Refugee Relief and Rehabilitation Department, through the Banglarbhumi website. Now you will have to submit copies of the registration deed, mutation certificate, receipt of the processing fee, fee acknowledgment number, copy of land and plot information and the map of the land that is getting converted, as well as a map of the adjacent land handy, before proceeding. Also keep the rent bills and electricity bills ready, as it might be needed in the process. Under the ‘Citizen Services’ tab, select the conversion application and proceed. Upload all the enclosures that are required to convert the land. Next, you need to make the online payment and complete the application. This is the legal procedure to apply for conversion.

  • Q: What is ROR in land records in West Bengal?
    A:

    West Bengal Record of Rights (ROR) is an extract from the land records register that is held by the Revenue Department of the State Government. ROR contains complete information regarding the land or the history of property and the ownership of that land. This document of revenue is an important document with legal status of the land or property. The West Bengal Record of Rights or Khatian register is maintained by every village separately in the Revenue Department. Government issues certified copies of the land records in the record of rights to the residents of West Bengal. 

    There is several importance of the West Bengal Record of Rights and they are as follows:

    • West Bengal Record of Rights or Khatian provides factual information on the real owner of the land.
    • The Khatian or this land record is beneficial to find out the false claim on the lands.
    • This land document which is registered in West Bengal record of rights can avoid the illegal means of land grabbing.
    • West Bengal record of rights will be an important evidence that can be used in the court litigations that are related to the property.
    • Record of Rights will help the legal land owner to be away from all the land oriented disputes in future.

    The major uses of this West Bengal Record of Rights are as follows:

    • It helps in the checking of ownership of an ancestral land or any other land.
    • Extracts of ROR or land records can be used to get the details on the type of land, and the variety of activities carried out on the land.
    • This West Bengal ROR is an important document to obtain the information related to any agricultural aspect of the land and its surrounding areas.
    • Khatian or the ROR is necessary in the 
    • Registrar’s office when sale transaction of the land or the mutation of land is done.
    • The record of rights in West Bengal is the most essential document for any landowner to raise a loan from a bank for the land.
    • In cases of civil litigation, the court demands for record of rights document on the land which is the certified copy of ROR.
    • This document is helpful in checking the legal ownership of the land while buying the land or property of a person.
    • Buyers will have to verify the revenue records of the land with ROR on which the flat is being constructed in case of buying flats. 

    The West Bengal Record of Rights contains the information in them as follows:

    • Details of all the changes in the ownership of land
    • Nature and limits of owner’s rights and condition
    • Mutation numbers
    • Details of the loan taken by the land occupant/owner
    • Type of irrigation whether it is irrigated kind or rain-fed kind
    • Information regarding the types of crops planted in the last cultivating season
    • Types of the soil whether agricultural or non- agricultural
    • Land or property survey number
    • Area of the earth that is fit for cultivation
    • Details of the charges of the attachments or decrees under the order of the civil court or the revenue authorities
    • Details on pending loans for buying seeds, pesticides or fertilizers
    • Details on pending litigations if there are any
    • The field of possession of each landholder and the classification of each area are entered from the Dag Chitha
    • Details regarding the paid and unpaid property taxes

    • Identification proof- Voter ID
    • Address proof- Aadhaar Card
    • Ration card
    • Proof of ownership of property

    • Encumbrance certificate
