How to Convert Meters to Feet?

Here are the key facts that you should know about Meters to Feet.

  • 1 meters is equal to 3.28084 foot or 1 meter into 3.28084 feet
  • 1 meter = (1/0.3048) foot
  • The distance of d in foot is equal to the d in meters, divided by 0.3048. Hence, the formula in such cases will be d (ft) = d (m) /0.3048.
  • Suppose 2 meters have to be converted to feet then 2m/0.3048 will be equal to 6.5617 foot.

What is Meter?

Before converting meters to feet, knowing a little more about the former is always recommended. The meter is the base unit for length as part of the SI or International System of Units. The m symbol is used to denote meter and it is often called the length of the specific path which is traversed by light when it is within a vacuum.

Here, the yard and inch are taken as their international units of measurement.

What is Feet?

Feet is something you have to carefully learn about when you are planning to convert meters to feet. This is a length unit and has been defined at 0.3048 meters in the year 1959. 1 foot has 12 inches while a yard has 3 feet.  The foot has been traditionally used as a system of measurement across the French, English, Chinese, Roman and Greek systems for long. It is still used worldwide for measuring land.

Current Use of Meters

Meters are SI (International System of Units) units for measuring length and they are used for several purposes including measuring length, height, distance and width among other parameters.

Current Use of Feet

Foot is a primary measurement unit in Canada, the United Kingdom and United States and is used for measuring height and altitude along with elevation as well.
